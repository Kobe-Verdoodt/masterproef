{Faith Kipyegon has spent her career redefining what’s possible on the track.



The world-record holder and three-time Olympic champion is one of the most accomplished middle-distance runners in history — and now, the Nike athlete is extending her impact as a mother and advocate.



Dedicated over the weekend at an event that included a cross-country kids race in Keringet, Nakuru County, Kenya, The Dare To Dream Maternity Ward in honor of Kipyegon reflects the runner’s long-standing commitment to women’s and maternal health. In a region where the nearest hospital is 25 to 35 kilometers away and emergency access is limited, the facility is intended to bring critical maternal care closer to home.



“For a long time, I carried a dream to create a place where women feel safe when they give life,” Kipyegon says. “Today, that dream becomes real. Together with Nike and this community, we are building a space where mothers are supported, respected and protected — and where life can begin with strength.”



As a mother herself, Kipyegon has spoken openly about why this work matters to her. Growing up in Keringet, she's seen women go into labor full of hope, carrying life and dreams, and too often return empty-handed — not because they failed, but because the care they needed was too far away or not good enough.



“This is not about me,” she says. “It’s about the women, the babies and the families who will leave here whole. When women are supported, communities rise.”



The maternity ward will include a dedicated maternity doctor, helping address geographic and infrastructural barriers that women and girls face when seeking care.



Tanya Hvizdak, Vice President of Global Running, was on the ground for the dedication. For her, the project reflects the reach athletes can have beyond competition.



“Faith Kipyegon is one of the greatest athletes of our time," Hvizdak says. “She carries her community with her, turning every victory into an opportunity to give back.
